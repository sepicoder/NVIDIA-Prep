How I Understand This Role

My Understanding of New Product Readiness

From my perspective, this role is less about managing a project schedule and more about ensuring NVIDIA is operationally ready to support a new product from engineering samples all the way through mass production and customer deployment.

The primary responsibility is creating readiness across people, processes, tooling, documentation, training, and infrastructure. That means identifying gaps early, managing dependencies across multiple teams, capturing lessons learned from previous launches, and making sure internal teams and customers are prepared before a product reaches scale.

What stands out to me is that readiness isn't just technical. It's also about communication, customer engagement, operational processes, and ensuring every stakeholder knows what they need to do for a successful launch. The TPM acts as the connective tissue across engineering, operations, solutions architecture, customers, and leadership to ensure nothing falls through the cracks.

While my background is primarily in software and enterprise platforms, I've done very similar work at SHAZAM. When I led the GitHub Copilot rollout and GitHub Enterprise Cloud migration, success wasn't just about the technology. It required risk assessment, readiness planning, documentation, training, stakeholder alignment, phased deployment, and post-launch support. The scale and technology are different, but the program management principles are very similar.

"I view this role as owning readiness, not just delivery. Delivery means the product ships. Readiness means NVIDIA and its customers can successfully adopt, support, and scale the product."
Fluid Requirements — My Approach

In highly innovative environments like NVIDIA, requirements often evolve as technology, customer needs, or technical discoveries emerge. I see program management as creating enough structure to enable innovation while still maintaining predictability and accountability. My job is not to lock things down — it's to build enough of a framework that the team can move fast without losing sight of what success looks like.

Ambiguity — How I Work Through It

In a fast-paced environment with ambiguity, I focus on bringing structure without slowing momentum. I clarify the objective and what success looks like even if the path isn't fully defined. I ask targeted questions to reduce uncertainty enough to move forward rather than waiting for perfect information. Then I break the work into small actionable steps and prioritize what delivers the most value first.

New Product Introduction (NPI) Experience

My NPI Story — GitHub Copilot as a Full NPI Lifecycle

Yes — I have New Product Introduction experience, especially through my work at SHAZAM.

The clearest example is when I led the enterprise rollout of GitHub Copilot. That program functioned like a full NPI cycle — taking a new AI developer tool from evaluation into production-scale adoption across 50+ engineers.

  • Risk assessment and enterprise approval — navigated full governance process before a single developer got access
  • Identity and access setup — Entra ID as IdP, SCIM provisioning, SSO configuration, eliminating manual SAF processing
  • Environment readiness — IDE validation across IntelliJ IDEA, Eclipse, VS Code, and Visual Studio
  • Phased rollout planning — one representative per team for pilot, defined go/no-go criteria before scaling
  • UAT-style feedback loops — short-form pulse surveys and long-form impact assessments as quality gates
  • Documentation and training — step-by-step onboarding guides per IDE environment
  • Post-launch continuous improvement — data residency compliance now being implemented as lessons learned

In parallel, I managed the GitHub Enterprise Cloud migration from our on-prem system — structured release planning, dependency management, clear entry/exit criteria across environments, and zero production incidents.

So while it was not hardware, it absolutely followed a full NPI-style lifecycle: from planning to pilot to production-scale adoption.
